Methodology

How LiveSafe works — data sources, classification logic, and known limitations.

What this system is

LiveSafe is a multi-dimensional area classification tool, not a predictive safety score. Each local authority is described across four independent dimensions — crime intensity, crime pattern, crime trend, and weather exposure — which are then combined into an overall area profile.

All classifications are relative within the 15-area prototype set. A label of "Elevated Crime Level" means elevated compared to the other areas in this prototype, not compared to all areas in England and Wales.

Data sources

Source What it provides Coverage
UK Police API Monthly street-level crime incidents, categorised by type and outcome Jan–Dec 2024
Meteostat Daily weather observations (temperature, precipitation) from nearest station Jan–Dec 2024
ONS Open Geography Portal Local authority boundary polygons (LAD 2024, EPSG:4326) 2024 boundaries

Classification dimensions

Crime Intensity — based on average monthly crime count across 2024. Thresholds are set at the 25th, 50th, and 75th percentiles of the 15-area sample.

LabelThreshold
Very Low Recorded CrimeBelow 25th percentile
Moderate Crime Level25th–50th percentile
Elevated Crime Level50th–75th percentile
Severe Crime ConcentrationAbove 75th percentile

Crime Pattern — based on the average share of violent and property crime categories across 2024 months.

LabelRule
Violence-DominantViolent share ≥ 30% and greater than property share
Property-Crime-DominantProperty share ≥ 30% and greater than violent share
Mixed Crime PatternBoth shares ≥ 20%
Low-Complexity Crime PatternNeither category strongly dominant

Crime Trend — based on the linear regression slope of monthly crime counts across 2024 (crimes per month change) and month-to-month variability.

LabelRule
ImprovingSlope < −0.5 and low variability
StableSlope between −0.5 and +0.5 and low variability
DeterioratingSlope > +0.5 and low variability
FluctuatingMonth-to-month variability above 75th percentile

Weather Exposure — based on average monthly precipitation, number of cold months (avg temp ≤ 5°C), and precipitation variability.

LabelRule
Mild ConditionsLow precipitation and ≤ 1 cold month
Rain-ExposedPrecipitation above 66th percentile
Cold-Exposed≥ 4 cold months
Seasonally VolatilePrecipitation variability above 75th percentile
High Weather StressBoth ≥ 4 cold months and high precipitation

Overall area profile derivation

The overall profile is derived from the four dimension labels through explicit conditional logic — not a weighted formula.

ProfileCondition
Persistent Multi-Factor Risk AreaHigh/severe crime AND adverse weather
Emerging Risk AreaHigh/severe crime AND deteriorating trend
Volatile Risk AreaHigh/severe crime AND fluctuating trend
Crime-Sensitive AreaHigh/severe crime (no other compounding factor)
Weather-Sensitive AreaAdverse weather AND low/moderate crime
Structurally Exposed AreaAdverse weather AND moderate-high crime
Stable Low-Risk AreaLow/moderate crime AND stable or improving trend
Mixed-Risk AreaAll other combinations

Limitations

Crime data from the UK Police API represents recorded crime only. Underreporting, policing priorities, and force-level recording practices all affect the numbers.

Weather data is sourced from the nearest Meteostat station to the LA centroid. For large or coastal LAs, the nearest station may not be representative of the whole area.

All percentile thresholds are computed within this 15-area prototype set. The labels are comparative — they describe each area relative to the others in this sample, not relative to the national distribution.

This system is designed for exploration and decision support. It is not a risk prediction engine and should not be used as the sole basis for any decision.
