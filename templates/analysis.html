K-Means Clustering

Unsupervised · 15 areas · K=4
K-Means groups the 15 areas into 4 clusters based purely on their crime and weather numbers — without being told the rule-based labels in advance. The scatter plot below shows how areas sit in 2D space (after dimension reduction using PCA). Areas that land in the same cluster share similar crime and weather profiles. If the data-driven clusters agree with the rule-based classification, that's validation that the classification system captures real patterns.

ML·03

Isolation Forest — Anomaly Detection

Unsupervised · 1,527 neighbourhoods · contamination 8%
While K-Means groups areas, Isolation Forest does something different — it hunts for neighbourhoods that don't fit any group. It works by asking: how easy is it to isolate this neighbourhood from all others using random cuts? The easier it is to isolate, the more unusual it is. A yellow dot on the Area Profile page marks these outlier neighbourhoods in the ranking table. For planners: anomalous neighbourhoods may warrant closer investigation — they exhibit crime patterns that are genuinely unusual for their area.

ML·04

Crime Trend Forecasting

Linear Regression · Train Jan–Oct · Test Nov–Dec
A linear regression model is trained on the first 10 months of 2024 crime data for each area, then tested on November–December which it hasn't seen. This gives us a genuine measure of predictive accuracy (MAE = Mean Absolute Error — lower is better). The model then projects 3 months forward with 95% confidence intervals. Wide confidence bands reflect the honest limitation of forecasting from just 12 data points.

ML·05

External Validation — IMD Correlation

Spearman ρ · ONS Index of Multiple Deprivation 2019
How do LiveSafe's crime intensity rankings compare to the government's own official measure? The ONS Index of Multiple Deprivation (IMD) 2019 includes a dedicated crime domain. Spearman's ρ measures whether the two ranking systems agree. A ρ close to 1.0 means they agree almost perfectly. A value above 0.6 is considered strong. This is a genuine external validation — the IMD was not used to build LiveSafe.
